Since the [[Division of the World]], the people of the [[T'va Islands]] have been the most rigorous in keeping meticulous records of their history. While most other islands let the story of the Oceansky fall into myth, a woman named [[Ceyi of T'va]] rose up as the first [[Storykeeper]] only two years after the Division, devoting her life to seeking out information and ensuring that future generations would continue her work. Ceyi saw that oral tradition could only be reliable up to a certain point and developed her own form of writing, creating the first T'va Scrolls. It is widely believed that these scrolls were the earliest appearance of an organized, universal system of written language.

# Transcriptions

## Scroll One
> The words of [[Ceyi of T'va]], the first [[Storykeeper]], recorded here for future generations to ponder and not forget. Let there in each generation rise up new Storykeepers who dedicate their lives to the preserving of our history. Let them study the old stories, speak with those who witness great events, and record the words of their time so that those who take their place may continue the tradition.
> Hear now the story of the [[Division of the World]], for a day may come when children are born who have never known of a world with one great land and a never-setting sun. This is the world from which all life has come, and it is the world all life must return to at the time of the [[Rejoining of the World]]. For all time until this one, humanity was gathered in one place under one [[Great Sun]]. The sun never set over the world, but constantly provided light, warmth, and life to the world. Humankind lived in peace with one another for untold ages. However, as humanity developed on the world, so did their greed, ambition, and vanity. Though the land and its resources were plentiful, people began to fight over ownership, which led to a time of great war. For one hundred years this evil age continued, until the land itself was so grieved by humanity's actions that it split itself into a thousand pieces so that humankind would not be altogether destroyed by one another. The ground shook and water came upon it, and the land became islands which were cast away from one another until they became only distant shapes on the edge of the sea. As they drifted from each other, they soon drifted from the sun. Humanity began to grow sick without the light and cried out that they were alone without help. So the sun showed mercy to the people and split itself into a thousand pieces, one for every island, and each smaller light provided warmth and life to a different piece of land. But humans grow arrogant easily, so to remind them what it was like to live without light, the sun's pieces dipped themselves into the sea to plunge the land in darkness at the end of every day. The lands drifted far and wide, drifting so far that they climbed into the water of the sky, their suns now calling out to one another from the dark. It is said that when the people of one land reach a peace and harmony akin to the way it was before the divide, their land will find another such people and their lights will be joined together, the people of each land now able to reach one another once again. But the sun can only wait for peace for so long. At the rejoining, when all of the land has drifted through the entirety of the sea and found its other parts once more, all of the lands which hold to evil ways and warring tendencies will be cast out, their light to leave them forever. But the lands which seek peace and goodness and mercy towards others will be joined together once more, never to live without the full light of the Great Sun again. These things are known because we have lived through them and the wise people of the islands have seen visions and heard whispers from the land as it tells its own story.
